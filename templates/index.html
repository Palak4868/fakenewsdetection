Related-Work

Fake News Early Detection: A Theory-driven Model.

Zhou et al, Jain et al, Phoha et al and Zafarani et al aimed to develop a theory-driven fake news detection model which concentrates on news content to detect earlier before it has been propagated on social media platforms. Their model represents the news by sets of features, capturing both structural and style language and using these features they built a machine learning model using supervised learning. The experimental result based on real-world dataset achieved the accuracy of 88%

Fake News Detection on Social Media: A Data Mining Perspective

Shu et al, Sliva et al Wang et al, Tang et al and Liu et al, In their paper to detect fake news on social media, a data mining perspective is presented that includes the characterization of fake news in psychology and social theories. There are two main factors that account for the widespread acceptance of fake messages by the user, which are naive realism and confirmatory bias. It proposes a general two-phase data mining framework that includes 1) feature extraction and 2) modelling, analysing data sets, and confusion matrix for identifying fake news.

Fake News or Truth? Using Satirical Cues to Detect Potentially Misleading News.

Rubin et al, Conroy et al, Chen et al and Cornwell et al, Proposed a ML model using SVM algorithm using five predictive features - Absurdity, Grammar Humour, Negative Affect and punctuations on number of news articles. The paper result reaches relatively high accuracy rate 87% by translating theories of humour, irony and satire into predictive method
